How to build a SMSF investment strategy in 2025

A self-managed super fund can be one of the most powerful wealth-building tools available to Australians — but only if you approach it with a clear, considered investment strategy.

What is an SMSF investment strategy?

An SMSF investment strategy is a written plan that sets out how your fund's assets will be invested to meet your retirement goals. Unlike retail or industry super funds, you as the trustee are responsible for creating, implementing, and regularly reviewing this strategy.

The ATO requires all SMSFs to have a documented investment strategy that covers risk, return, liquidity, diversification, and the ability to meet fund liabilities. Failing to maintain an appropriate strategy can result in compliance issues and penalties.

Key components of a strong SMSF strategy

  • Risk tolerance: Define how much volatility your fund can absorb based on member age, time to retirement, and income needs.
  • Asset allocation: Determine the mix of Australian shares, international equities, property, fixed income, and cash.
  • Diversification: Avoid over-concentration in any single asset class or investment.
  • Liquidity: Ensure you can meet pension payments and other fund expenses at all times.
Case Study

A Melbourne couple in their mid-50s restructured their SMSF portfolio from 80% Australian shares to a diversified mix including international ETFs and direct property — reducing volatility by 28% while maintaining long-term growth targets.
